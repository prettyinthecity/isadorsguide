Easy Mile High Bread

(Converted recipe from the video)

Ingredients

Whole Wheat/All Purpose Flour Bread

  • 435g of water
  • 2.125 of yeast
  • 500g of all purpose flour
  • 10g of salt
  • 50g of whole wheat flour

All Purpose Flour Bread

  • 435g of water
  • 2.125 of yeast
  • 500g of all purpose flour
  • 10g of salt

Equipment

  • A bowl
  • Plastic wrap
  • A spoon
  • A oven
  • Two dough scrapers
  • Parchment paper
  • A half sheet tray

Method

Poolish

  1. Stir 150g of room temperature water, ⅛ teaspoon OR a small pinch of yeast, and 150g of all purpose flour with a spoon till it is semi-runny and sticky. If poolish is too dry, and ½ a tablespoon of water until it is semi-runny and sticky
  2. Put plastic wrap OR damp cloth over the bowl for however you would like your crust. Let sit for 48 hours for nice crust, 24 hours for “so-so” crust, and 4-16 hours for basic bread

Making The Dough

  1. Stir 280g of water (98 degrees fahrenheit), 2g of yeast, and the poolish from before with a spoon
  2. Slowly stir in 400g of all purpose flour and 10g of salt with a spoon OR 350g of all purpose flour, 50g of whole wheat flour, and 10g of salt for a different flavor with a spoon
  3. Wet your hands and do 60-90 seconds of squeeze, rotate, and repeat
  4. Put plastic wrap over the bowl and let sit for 30 minutes
  5. Wet your hands again and 8-9 scoop, pull as much as you can, and then place the pulled portion of the dough on top of the portion already in the bowl
  6. 6-7 scoop it from underneath, turn it 90 degrees and fold it on itself
  7. Put plastic wrap over the bowl and let sit for 30 minutes again
  8. Repeat steps 5, 6, and 7
  9. Put plastic wrap over the bowl and let sit for 1 hour
  10. Preheat the oven to 525 degrees fahrenheit

Shaping and Cooking the Dough

  1. Flip the dough onto a lightly floured workspace, lightly flour the dough, and grab a half sheet tray and line it with parchment
  2. Cut the dough in half with a dough scraper
  3. Tuck the ends of the dough masses, fluff it, take each corner and put it in the middle and put the seam face side down.
  4. Put the dough on the half sheet tray
  5. Spray the loaves with a little water
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes or when the dough gets brown and crispy

Notes: Usually the parchment will burn and your home will start smoking up. Don't be alarmed, this is how things are supposed to be. Just crack a window. I also suggest if your bread loaf isn’t looking brown enough, you should do five minute increments. To tailor your recipe to your altitude, experiment and record your new numbers in a notebook.
